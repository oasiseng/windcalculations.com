Ke — Elevation Factor Note
ASCE 7-22 §26.9 includes a ground elevation factor (Ke) that reduces wind pressures at altitude to account for lower air density. At 5,000 ft elevation (typical Boulder), Ke ≈ 0.83 — meaning velocity pressures are ~83% of sea-level values. The 2024 IBC incorporates Ke; however, the 2024 IRC does not. Per SEAC (2025), an equivalent sea-level wind speed Ve = V√Ke may be used with IRC tables. Ke use is optional and subject to AHJ approval.
