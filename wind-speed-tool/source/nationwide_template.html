Ke Elevation Factor Note

ASCE 7-22 Section 26.9 allows a ground elevation factor Ke that can reduce wind pressures at higher elevations because of lower air density. The 2024 IBC incorporates Ke; project teams should still verify local adoption and AHJ acceptance. Front Range and other mountain sites should confirm whether an elevation adjustment is appropriate before converting Vult into design pressures.

Data & Method Notes

This page first queries the public ASCE 7-22 GIS raster layers for the mapped value at the selected point. If the live service is unavailable, it falls back to the embedded simplified contour geometry and local interpolation. Colorado Front Range points additionally cross-reference the SEAC/CPP overlay contained in the Boulder reference implementation.
